# Airport Notes

Airports compress humanity: tired parents, people crying at arrivals, someone treating a €9 sandwich like a betrayal.

I like the hour before boarding. Phones half-dead. Everyone pretending they are fine. I write here because **transit is honest** — you are literally between places, which is a metaphor too on-the-nose to ignore.

Today: remembering that most problems I left at home will still fit in my carry-on if I insist on bringing them.
