# The Weight of Absence

There is a particular silence that follows a video call when it ends — not empty, but **full of everything you did not say**.

I used to think love needed proximity to stay real. I was wrong. Proximity makes love easier to perform. Distance reveals what is actually there.

## What distance taught me

- **Small rituals matter more** — the good morning message, the shared song, the photo of ordinary Tuesday light
- **Jealousy is often loneliness in disguise** — name it honestly before it becomes a weapon
- **Planning the next meeting** is its own kind of hope

## A line I keep

> We are not failing at closeness. We are practicing a different shape of it.

I write this not as advice, but as a record — so future me remembers that tenderness can survive geography when both people choose it daily.
