# On Silence

Silence is not the absence of sound. It is the **absence of urgency**.

I spent years filling gaps — podcasts while walking, music while working, scrolling while waiting. The gaps were not empty. They were where unprocessed life accumulated.

## Three kinds of quiet

1. **External quiet** — turning notifications off (necessary but shallow)
2. **Internal quiet** — when the mind stops rehearsing arguments
3. **Sacred quiet** — rare; feels like room expanding

## Practice

I do not meditate perfectly. I sit for ten minutes some mornings and fail with dignity. The point is not enlightenment. The point is remembering that I am not only what I produce.

> Before you ask what to add to your life, ask what you can stop defending.
